Hello there! I am an engineer and researcher in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), currently on sabbatical to focus on my open-source project edge264.

My professional background is in Graphical User Interfaces (GUI) and software optimization:

  • Engineering degree in HCI and Competitive Programming from Centrale Marseille and KTH in Sweden (2008 â€“ 2012)
  • R&D engineer in Paris on developping the software for an embedded digital TV recorder (2012 â€“ 2014)

  • Research engineer on GUI for territorial concertations in the SENS and SELMET teams at INRAE and CIRAD in Montpellier (2022 â€“ 2024)

I really enjoy crafting innovations in software, making nice talks to share them, and bringing them all the way to end users. My main achievements so far have been:

  • edge264, an open-source H.264/AVC video decoder improving speed and code size over the state of the art, to challenge programming practices in the Open Media community [FOSDEM'24, FOSDEM'25] and help further reduce the energy footprint of video playback
  • Polyphony, a toolkit demonstrating the building of graphical user interfaces with the Entity-Component-System model, along with an award-winning article

Being very concerned about ecology, I have been a willing member of the Sustainable Development and Societal Responsibility local committee at Centrale Lyon, and have co-chaired the French working group on HCI and ecology. I work hard to explore unconventional paths, most of my spare time going into compulsively crafting code then sharing it with engineering communities.
